# Distributive Laws

$$

A \cup (B \cap C) = (A \cup B) \cap (A \cup B)
A \cap (B \cup C) = (A \cap B) \cup (A \cap C)

$$

## Proof

$$

A \cup (B \cap C) = (A \cup B) \cap (A \cup B)

$$

We must prove that $A \cup (B \cap C) \subset (A \cup B) \cap (A \cup C)$

# Morgans Laws

$$

(A \cup B)^c = A^c \cap B^c
(A \cap B)^c = A^c \cup B^c

$$
